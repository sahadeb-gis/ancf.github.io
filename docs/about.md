# About ANCF

## Who We Are

**Avayaranya Nature Conservation Foundation (ANCF)** — *অভয়ারণ্য* — is a
nonprofit organisation founded in **2013** as a consortium of foresters and
environment enthusiasts. We work across Bangladesh on the conservation of
endangered tree species and native fruit species, biodiversity habitat
restoration, and climate action.

The name *Avayaranya* (অভয়ারণ্য) means "sanctuary" in Bengali — a place
where life is safe. That is what we try to build, one campus, one school,
one hill village at a time.

---

## Mission

To conserve Bangladesh's native and endangered tree species, restore
biodiversity habitats, and engage communities in climate action through
plantation, mapping, applied research, and environmental education.

## Vision

A Bangladesh where native species, their habitats, and the communities that
live alongside them thrive together — supported by science, sustained by
local stewardship, and resilient to climate change.

## Core Values

- **Native first.** We prioritise indigenous and endangered species over
  ornamental or exotic plantings.
- **Community-led.** Every project is anchored by local coordinators,
  schools, or institutions — not parachuted in.
- **Evidence-based.** We track what we plant, where, and what survives,
  and we use that data to plan the next season.
- **Open.** Our project history, maps, and field notes are shared so others
  can build on them.

## What We Do

ANCF works across nine interconnected domains:

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-tree:{ .lg .middle } **Community Plantation**

    Native and endangered tree species planted with local communities.

-   :material-weather-sunny:{ .lg .middle } **Climate Actions**

    Plantation, awareness, and advocacy linked to climate resilience.

-   :material-tag-multiple:{ .lg .middle } **Tree Tagging**

    Labelling individual trees to support identification and monitoring.

-   :material-bird:{ .lg .middle } **Biodiversity Conservation**

    Protecting habitats for native flora and fauna.

-   :material-account-group:{ .lg .middle } **Community Awareness**

    Schools, fairs, campuses, and youth engagement programmes.

-   :material-leaf:{ .lg .middle } **Forest Bathing**

    Guided nature immersion to rebuild a human–forest connection.

-   :material-map-search:{ .lg .middle } **Mapping**

    Spatial inventories of endangered plants and habitats.

-   :material-flask-outline:{ .lg .middle } **Science in Conservation**

    Quantitative tools (i-Tree, remote sensing) applied to field work.

-   :material-pine-tree:{ .lg .middle } **Nature Experience**

    Field visits and learning programmes for students and the public.

</div>

## Where We Work

ANCF projects since 2013 have spanned at least the following districts of
Bangladesh:

- **Dhaka division:** Dhaka, Munshiganj, Madaripur, Tangail, Faridpur,
  Rajbari, Kishoreganj
- **Chittagong division:** Chattogram (incl. Sandwip), Bandarban, Rangamati,
  Cumilla
- **Sylhet division:** Sylhet
- **Barisal division:** Barisal, Pirojpur, Patuakhali

A detailed year-by-year project list is available in the
[ANCF project history PDF](assets/images/ancf_%20project%20history.pdf).

## History at a Glance

<div class="timeline" markdown>

<div class="timeline-entry" markdown>

### 2013 — Founded
*Dhaka, Bangladesh*

- Avayaranya is formed as a consortium of foresters and environment
  enthusiasts.
- First projects in Munshiganj, Tejgaon (Dhaka), Madaripur, and Dhaka

<div class="timeline-entry" markdown>

*Multiple districts*

- Projects scale to schools and colleges across Kishoreganj, Cumilla,
  Bandarban, Faridpur, Munshiganj, and Madaripur.
- First hill-tract plantation in Ruma, Bandarban.

<div class="timeline-entry" markdown>

### 2017–2018 — Campus and Coastal Reach
*Sylhet, Barisal, Sandwip*

- Plantation and awareness programmes at SUST (Sylhet), multiple Barisal
  institutions, and Sandwip island (Chattogram).
- Tree-tagging initiatives gain traction on university campuses.

*National*

  pilots. Book-fair and campus engagement continue annually.
